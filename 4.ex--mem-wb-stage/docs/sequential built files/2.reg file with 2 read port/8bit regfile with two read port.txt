

# Manual 8-Bit Register File Implementation (4x8) for ID/EX Stage

## 1. Overview and Components

This document details the complete implementation of an 8-bit wide, 4-register file for the ID/EX stage of a processor. Since the 74LS670 register file IC is unavailable in Proteus, we construct an equivalent 4x8 register file (4 registers, each 8 bits wide) using standard logic ICs. The design provides separate read and write addressing for efficient processor operation.

**Required Components:**
- **Eight** 74LS173 Quad D Flip-Flops (for 32 bits of storage: 4 registers × 8 bits)
- One 74LS139 Dual 2-to-4 Line Decoder (for decoding write addresses)
- **Four** 74LS153 Dual 4-to-1 Multiplexers (for selecting the 8-bit read data)
- Standard Power (VCC, GND) and input/output components

**IC Naming Convention:**
To make wiring clear, we will name the eight 74LS173 ICs as follows:
*   **REG0_L:** Holds the lower 4 bits (D0-D3) of Register 0
*   **REG0_H:** Holds the upper 4 bits (D4-D7) of Register 0
*   **REG1_L:** Holds the lower 4 bits (D0-D3) of Register 1
*   **REG1_H:** Holds the upper 4 bits (D4-D7) of Register 1
*   **REG2_L:** Holds the lower 4 bits (D0-D3) of Register 2
*   **REG2_H:** Holds the upper 4 bits (D4-D7) of Register 2
*   **REG3_L:** Holds the lower 4 bits (D0-D3) of Register 3
*   **REG3_H:** Holds the upper 4 bits (D4-D7) of Register 3

## 3. Write Logic Implementation

### 3.1 Common 8-Bit Data Bus Connections
The 8-bit data bus (D7-D0) must be connected to the inputs of all eight 74LS173 ICs simultaneously.

**Lower Nibble Bus (D3-D0):**
*   Connect **D0** to Pin 14 of **REG0_L, REG1_L, REG2_L, and REG3_L**.
*   Connect **D1** to Pin 13 of **REG0_L, REG1_L, REG2_L, and REG3_L**.
*   Connect **D2** to Pin 12 of **REG0_L, REG1_L, REG2_L, and REG3_L**.
*   Connect **D3** to Pin 11 of **REG0_L, REG1_L, REG2_L, and REG3_L**.

**Upper Nibble Bus (D7-D4):**
*   Connect **D4** to Pin 14 of **REG0_H, REG1_H, REG2_H, and REG3_H**.
*   Connect **D5** to Pin 13 of **REG0_H, REG1_H, REG2_H, and REG3_H**.
*   Connect **D6** to Pin 12 of **REG0_H, REG1_H, REG2_H, and REG3_H**.
*   Connect **D7** to Pin 11 of **REG0_H, REG1_H, REG2_H, and REG3_H**.

### 3.2 Write Selection (74LS139 Decoder)
The 74LS139 decoder selects which register pair to write to. **Each decoder output must connect to BOTH the low and high nibble registers.**

| 74LS139 Pin | Signal Name | Connects to Enable Pins (IE1' & IE2') of... |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Pin 4 | Y0' | **REG0_L** (Pins 9 & 10) AND **REG0_H** (Pins 9 & 10) |
| Pin 5 | Y1' | **REG1_L** (Pins 9 & 10) AND **REG1_H** (Pins 9 & 10) |
| Pin 6 | Y2' | **REG2_L** (Pins 9 & 10) AND **REG2_H** (Pins 9 & 10) |
| Pin 7 | Y3' | **REG3_L** (Pins 9 & 10) AND **REG3_H** (Pins 9 & 10) |

**Note:** For each of the eight 74LS173 ICs, bridge its Pin 9 and Pin 10 together so the single decoder output can control both.

### 3.3 Control Bus for Write Operations
- **Clock Pulse (CP):** Connect Pin 7 of all **eight** 74LS173 ICs (REG0_L to REG3_H) to a single common clock source.
- **Master Reset (MR):** Connect Pin 15 of all **eight** 74LS173 ICs to a single reset switch (tie to GND for normal operation).
- **Output Enable (OE1 & OE2):** Connect Pin 1 and Pin 2 of all **eight** 74LS173 ICs permanently to ground (GND). This ensures their outputs are always driving the multiplexer inputs.

## 4. Read Logic Implementation

The read logic requires **four** 74LS153 multiplexers to produce an 8-bit output. Each MUX handles two bits of the final output.

### 4.1 Mux IC Assignments
- **MUX 1:** Provides outputs **Q0** and **Q1**
- **MUX 2:** Provides outputs **Q2** and **Q3**
- **MUX 3:** Provides outputs **Q4** and **Q5**
- **MUX 4:** Provides outputs **Q6** and **Q7**

|--------|---------------------------------------------|
| Read Address RA0 | Pin 14 (A) |
| Read Address RA1 | Pin 2 (B) |
| Read Enable RE' | Pin 1 (1E) and Pin 15 (2E) |

### 4.7 Final 8-Bit Output
The final 8-bit output (Q7-Q0) is taken from the outputs of the four multiplexers:
- **Q0:** Pin 7 (1Y) of **MUX 1**
- **Q1:** Pin 9 (2Y) of **MUX 1**
- **Q2:** Pin 7 (1Y) of **MUX 2**
- **Q3:** Pin 9 (2Y) of **MUX 2**
- **Q4:** Pin 7 (1Y) of **MUX 3**
- **Q5:** Pin 9 (2Y) of **MUX 3**
- **Q6:** Pin 7 (1Y) of **MUX 4**
- **Q7:** Pin 9 (2Y) of **MUX 4**

## 5. Operation Guide

### 5.1 Writing 8-Bit Data to a Register
1.  Set the 8-bit data you want to write on the common data bus (D7-D0).
2.  Set the 2-bit write address (WA0, WA1) to select the target register (e.g., `00` for REG0, `01` for REG1, etc.).
3.  Ensure the Master Reset (MR) is LOW for normal operation.
4.  Apply a positive edge to the Clock Pulse (CP). The data on the bus will be written into the selected register pair (e.g., for address `00`, data is written to REG0_L and REG0_H).

### 5.2 Reading 8-Bit Data from a Register
1.  Set the Read Enable (RE') signal to LOW (active).
2.  Set the 2-bit read address (RA0, RA1) to select the source register.
3.  The 8-bit output will appear on the Q7-Q0 pins as defined in section 4.7.

## 6. Troubleshooting Tips

1.  **Incorrect Output Data (e.g., wrong bits, swapped nibbles):** This is the most common issue. Systematically verify the 32 connections between the 8 register outputs (Q0-Q3) and the 32 multiplexer inputs (1X0-2X3) across all four MUXes. Double-check sections 4.2 through 4.5. Ensure REGX_H outputs go to MUX 3 & 4, and REGX_L outputs go to MUX 1 & 2.
2.  **Unable to Write Data:** Verify that each decoder output (from the 74LS139) is correctly connected to **both** the low and high nibble registers for that address. Check that the clock signal is reaching all 8 registers. Ensure the write address (WA0, WA1) is connected to the 74LS139's address pins.
3.  **No Output:** Ensure the Read Enable (RE') is LOW and is correctly wired to Pins 1 and 15 of all four MUXes. Confirm that the OE pins (1 & 2) on all 74LS173s are tied to GND.
4.  **Only Lower or Upper Nibble Outputs Work:** You have likely miswired the control signals. Check that RA0 and RA1 are connected to **all four** MUXes. Check that the Read Enable (RE') is connected to **all four** MUXes.
